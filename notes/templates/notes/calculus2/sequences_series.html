1. Sequences

A sequence is an ordered list of numbers \( \{a_n\} \) where each term has a specific position.

Convergence of Sequences

A sequence converges to \( L \) if:

$$ \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = L $$

If the limit exists and is finite, the sequence converges. Otherwise, it diverges.

💡
Monotonic Sequence Theorem: Every bounded, monotonic sequence is convergent.

Example: Sequence Convergence

Determine the convergence of \( a_n = \frac{n}{n+1} \).

Solution:

$$ \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{n}{n+1} = \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{1 + 1/n} = 1 $$

The sequence converges to 1.

2. Infinite Series

An infinite series is the sum of a sequence: \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n \).

Definition
$$ S = \lim_{k \to \infty} S_k $$

where \( S_k = \sum_{n=1}^{k} a_n \) is the \( k \)-th partial sum.

Geometric Series

The series \( \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} ar^n \) converges if \( |r| < 1 \) and diverges if \( |r| \ge 1 \).

$$ S = \frac{a}{1-r} \quad \text{for } |r| < 1 $$

Example: Geometric Series

Find the sum of \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} 5\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{n-1} \).

Solution: \( a=5, r=2/3 \). Since \( |2/3| < 1 \), it converges.

$$ S = \frac{5}{1 - 2/3} = \frac{5}{1/3} = 15 $$

3. Convergence Tests

Divergence Test

If \( \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n \neq 0 \), then \( \sum a_n \) diverges.

Integral Test

If \( f(x) \) is continuous, positive, and decreasing on \( [1, \infty) \) and \( a_n = f(n) \):

\( \sum a_n \) converges if and only if \( \int_{1}^{\infty} f(x) \, dx \) converges.

Example: Integral Test

Test \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2 + 1} \).

Solution:

$$ \int_{1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{x^2+1} \, dx = \lim_{t \to \infty} [\tan^{-1} x]_1^t = \frac{\pi}{2} - \frac{\pi}{4} = \frac{\pi}{4} $$

The integral converges, so the series converges.

Comparison Tests
  • Direct Comparison: If \( 0 \le a_n \le b_n \) and \( \sum b_n \) converges, then \( \sum a_n \) converges.
  • Limit Comparison: If \( \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{a_n}{b_n} = c > 0 \) (finite), then both series converge or both diverge.

Example: Limit Comparison Test

Test \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{n-1}{2n^2 + 3} \).

Solution: Compare with \( b_n = \frac{1}{2n} \) (Harmonic series, divergent).

$$ \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{(n-1)/(2n^2+3)}{1/2n} = \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{2n^2 - 2n}{2n^2 + 3} = 1 > 0 $$

Both diverge.

Ratio Test

Let \( L = \lim_{n \to \infty} \left| \frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n} \right| \).

  • If \( L < 1 \), the series converges absolutely.
  • If \( L > 1 \), the series diverges.
  • If \( L = 1 \), the test is inconclusive.

Example: Ratio Test

Test \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{n^2}{2^n} \).

Solution:

$$ \left| \frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n} \right| = \frac{(n+1)^2}{2^{n+1}} \cdot \frac{2^n}{n^2} = \frac{1}{2} \left(\frac{n+1}{n}\right)^2 \to \frac{1}{2} < 1 $$

The series converges.

Root Test

Let \( L = \lim_{n \to \infty} \sqrt[n]{|a_n|} \).

  • If \( L < 1 \), converges absolutely.
  • If \( L > 1 \), diverges.
  • If \( L = 1 \), inconclusive.

4. Power Series

A power series is a series of the form:

$$ \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} c_n (x - a)^n $$

It has a Radius of Convergence (R) and an Interval of Convergence found using the Ratio Test.

💡
Remember: Always check convergence at the endpoints of the interval separately!

5. Taylor and Maclaurin Series

Functions can be represented as power series.

Taylor Series centered at \( a \):
$$ f(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{f^{(n)}(a)}{n!} (x - a)^n $$
Maclaurin Series (Taylor Series with \( a = 0 \))
$$ f(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{f^{(n)}(0)}{n!} x^n $$
Common Maclaurin Series
\( e^x = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^n}{n!} = 1 + x + \frac{x^2}{2!} + \frac{x^3}{3!} + \dots \)
\( \sin x = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-1)^n \frac{x^{2n+1}}{(2n+1)!} = x - \frac{x^3}{3!} + \frac{x^5}{5!} - \dots \)
\( \cos x = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-1)^n \frac{x^{2n}}{(2n)!} = 1 - \frac{x^2}{2!} + \frac{x^4}{4!} - \dots \)

Example: Finding a Maclaurin Series

Find the Maclaurin series for \( f(x) = \cos x \).

Solution: Derivatives at 0: \( \cos(0)=1, -\sin(0)=0, -\cos(0)=-1, \sin(0)=0, \cos(0)=1 \dots \)

Coefficients: \( 1, 0, -1/2!, 0, 1/4! \dots \)

$$ \cos x = 1 - \frac{x^2}{2!} + \frac{x^4}{4!} - \dots = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-1)^n \frac{x^{2n}}{(2n)!} $$
